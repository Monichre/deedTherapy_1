With years of experience working alongside men and women of all ages, backgrounds, and cultures in high schools and colleges and businesses, trained in several healing/transformative modalities to complement the traditional techniques gained from a Masters in Mental Health Counseling, I offer a variety of complementary approaches to address the changes you seek and those you’ve yet to discover.
An Introduction

I will be unrelenting in the support I offer you, recognizing that we share this experience as described by Rudolf Steiner: “Every condition in the soul not in harmony with the highest ideals is disturbed and interrupted again and again.” In the near daily fluctuations through excitement and boredom and despair and joy one can feel desperately confused and alone. Through our discussions, developing relaxation and awareness, I emphasize getting familiar and comfortable with that self which confronts all experience. I will teach methods and exercises to strengthen the three main parts of your being through which you understand yourself in the world: thinking, feeling, and willing.

Seeking Therapy

Seeking therapy indicates an acknowledgment that there’s some interference going on with the business of living.
