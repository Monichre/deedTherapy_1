Mark's Story

Mark Wildermann has over twenty years of experience in the educational field (mostly at the college level in both student development and academic departments) and is well grounded in life experience. At the age of seventeen, he dropped out of college after three days to work on a freighter; returning to school he was baffled in his freshman year by the introductory psychology course which presented in all seriousness behaviorism as explanation of the human being; several years later he visited the Bollingen Tower in Switzerland of the founder of analytical psychology Carl Jung; graduating with a B.A. in English he wrote plays and screenplays while working as a baker and truck driver among other things. He earned an M.Ed. in Mental Health which is balanced by training and practice in several alternative healing modalities: Transcendental Meditation, Anthroposophy, Reiki Master Teacher, Past Life Regression Therapist, Bach Flower Therapist, and Nationally Certified Hypnotherapist. I have worked with the most diverse individuals and groups at all stages of personal development. The unceasing rewards and challenges of those relationships have taught me to adapt techniques and treatments for the unique situation that’s always presented. No stone left unturned. No client left unrecognized.

His Approach

I will be unrelenting in the support I offer you, recognizing that we share this experience as described by Rudolf Steiner: “Every condition in the soul not in harmony with the highest ideals is disturbed and interrupted again and again.” In the near daily fluctuations through excitement and boredom and despair and joy one can feel desperately confused and alone. Through our discussions, developing relaxation and awareness, I emphasize getting familiar and comfortable with that self which confronts all experience. I will teach methods and exercises to strengthen the three main parts of your being through which you understand yourself in the world: thinking, feeling, and willing.

Philosophy

Seeking therapy indicates an acknowledgment that the experience of distress and exclusion interferes with the business of living. To responsibly ask for counsel is a healthy response to the world, a normal inclination, and a courageous deed..

“A moment of action in oneself is worth an age of apprehension through others; not that our deeds are better, but that they produce a renewal of our being.”

It signifies one’s decision to best do one’s part as a member of this human community. Carl Rogers’ foundational practice of unconditional positive regard along with traditional techniques such as solution-based therapy and paradoxical intention are complemented by relaxation and mindfulness training through hypnotherapy and the inclusion of individually selected Bach Flower preparations. Follow-up sessions after initial meetings can be done for convenience of time and cost through phone consultations.
